# PF2e Fauna rules

In this game you will need 1 game master to run the thing, and as many players as you want (at like 4-5 it slogs down a lot, 3 felt like the magic number).

The point of the game is to guess different statistics of creatures simply based on their pictures, names, and your intuition. The game rules were heavily inspired by the boardgame [Fauna](https://boardgamegeek.com/boardgame/35497/fauna).

The point of the game is to gather the most points and be the first to reach x points (something you agree on beforehand). You gain points by guessing statistics correctly or at least *well enough*, while incorrect guesses hinder your next turn.

## How to play
The game is composed of rounds, within each round each player has a turn. How the turn order is set is up to you, a very simple one is to just use sitting order(/name order in the dicord call), and just rotate who is the first guesser each round. But you can also roll for initiative or anything else you come up with.

Each round is 1 creature that is curated by the GM, and on each turn the active player has to make 1 guess. If you do not want to make a guess you can "pass" instead, but that means you are not allowed to make any more guesses this round. The others (even if just 1 person) continue until they run out of guesses or pass.

Each player can make up to 5 guesses each round, however this might be lowered temporarily from missed guesses (will talk about it in a moment). There are set things you can guess: size, level, one of it's traits, it's rarity, special speeds, and if the GM can discern it then plane of existence.

Guesses made are public, and once something is guessed (for example that a certain creature is level 12), nobody is allowed to guess that exact same thing again in the same round (but they might guess that it is level 11). Furthermore, each guess category mentiones whether the same player can put in multiple guesses, this is only a limit to that one player (for example you can guess multiple traits, but not multiple sizes).

After everyone has passed and/or ran out of guesses, the GM reveals the true values of the creature, and you tally up points for the round. How many points each guess is worth is at the later half of this rule document. For each guess *made* that *didn't earn any points* you lose one guess for the following round. So if you had 2 guesses that you made, but didn't earn you points, you will only be able to guess 3 times in the next round (if let's say all 3 of those hit, then the round after that you have 5 again, these guesses are only locked out for 1 round specifically).

Once all points and misses are tallied check if someone reached the point treshold of the game. If yes than you have a winner, if not, continue to next round.

I usually play with 80 points as the treshold, but you can move this up or down for longer or shorter games. Do publicly agree on this before you start!
### GM side
You will need to do the following things:
- Curate creatures
- Keep track of guesses
- Tally up points
- Keep track of missed guesses
#### Curating creatures
Open AoN, in filters remove humanoids (as they make the game very boring), and in options (the row below filters) go to sorting and click "random". Here is an already set up [link](https://2e.aonprd.com/Creatures.aspx?exclude-traits=humanoid&sort=random&display=table&columns=creature_family+source+rarity+size+trait+level+hp+ac+fortitude+reflex+will+perception+sense+speed) for it. To get a new assortment of random creatures you to to options -> sorting and there is a button called "new seed" that will reroll the order.
Find the first creature that has a picture (use some of your own discretion to weed out ones that are too boring, although simple or easy ones can still be very fun and fine to add!!!). Once you have your creature, check how many traits it has that are in the guessable traits list, and check if you can from a quick glance find out which plane of existence it is from (just make a very quick read-through of it's lore above and below the statblock). Do not guess a plane for it if you don't know for absolute certain!

Once you have all the info, present the following to your players: the creature's name, it's image, how many traits of it can be guessed, and whether they can guess a plane of existence for it (material plane is not guessable cus that didn't really work out when I ran it).
#### Keep track of guesses
Let the round begin and just write and edit a message all your players can see with like:
- Bob: medium size, level 12, (passed)
- Alice: rare creture, dragon traits
- Charlie: burrowing speed, animal trait

In this phase do not verify if they guessed correctly or not, just write it up.
#### Tally up points & track missed guesses
Once the round ends (everyone ran out of guesses and/or passed) reveal the creature statistics, and tally up each person's points and add it to their total. Also check how many they missed and write it up somewhere so everyone knows how many guesses they have for next round (each miss is -1 guess for next round only)

## Points
### Size
(can only guess __one__ per player per round)
- Exact hit: 4 points
- Adjacent hit: 1 point
> What counts as adjacent: if the correct size is ``medium``, adjacent would be ``small`` or ``large``.
> All existing sizes are in order:
> - Tiny (example: cat)
> - Small (example: mastiff, eagle)
> - Medium (example: wolf, human)
> - Large (example: horse)
> - Huge (example: elephant, killer whale)
> - Gargantuan (example: blue whale)
### Level
(can only guess __one__ per player per round)
- Exact hit: 10 points
- Adjacent hit: 3 points (range of 3)
> What counts as adjacent: if the correct level is ``17``, then the "adjacent" levels would be ``14-16`` and ``18-20``.
>
> The full pf2e level range is -1 to 25.
### Traits
(can  guess __multiple__ per player per round)
> Writers notes: these traits are partially organized for what worked well for my newbie-ish play group, you might want to move around stuff like archon, or mummy, etc.
>
> Basically the points should reflect: rarity, and how easy it is to guess on a creature.
- 5 point traits: Aberration, Amphibious, Animal, Aquatic, Beast, Celestial, Construct, Dragon, Dinosaur, Elemental, Fey, Fiend, Fungus, Incorporeal, Ooze, Plant, Skeleton, Swarm, Troll, Undead
- 7 point traits: Acid, Air, Angel, Cold, Daemon, Demon, Devil, Earth, Electricity, Fire, Genie, Ghost, Ghoul, Giant, Holy, Metal, Mindless, Monitor, Mummy, Nymph, Poison, Spirit, Sprite, Unholy, Water, Wood
- 9 point traits: Arcane, Archon, Astral, Darvakka, Divine, Dream, Ethereal, Golem, Gremlin, Mutant, Mythic, Occult, Phantom, Primal, Protean, Psychopomp, Qlippoth, Sahkil, Tane, Time
### Rarity traits
(can only guess __one__ per player per round)
- Common (1 point)
- Uncommon (4 points)
- Rare (7 points)
- Unique (10 points)
### Special Speeds
(can  guess __multiple__ per player per round)
> Writer's notes: this is my newest addition and the point values might be a bit too high, only played with speeds once as of the writing of this.
- Burrow: 4 points
- Climb: 3 points
- Fly: 3 points
- Swim: 4 points
### Plane of Origin
(can only guess __one__ per player per round)
The Material Plane cannot be guessed.
- Exact: 8 points
- Within the Group: 2 points
